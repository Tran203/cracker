SERVICES

Women's Health

Obstetricians/Gynecologists (OB/GYNs): These doctors specialize in women's reproductive health and provide services such as prenatal care, family planning, and gynecological care. Nurse Practitioners and Midwives: These healthcare providers may also offer women's health services, including prenatal care and family planning.

Child Health

Pediatrician: Pediatricians specialize in the healthcare of infants, children, and adolescents. They provide vaccinations, well-child check-ups, and treatment for childhood illnesses. Family Physicians: Family doctors often provide primary care to children as well as adults.

HIV, AIDS, Tuberculosis, and STDs

Patient-Centric Care: We prioritize the needs, preferences, and well-being of patients in all that we do, ensuring that their healthcare experience is at the center of our focus. Innovation: We embrace innovation as a means to continuously improve our services, leveraging technology and creative solutions to enhance healthcare accessibility and efficiency. Empowerment: We empower patients by providing them with the tools and information they need to make informed decisions about their healthcare, fostering a sense of control and autonomy.

Basic Antenatal Care

Obstetricians/Gynecologists (OB/GYNs): OB/GYNs provide prenatal care, monitor pregnancies, and assist with childbirth. Certified Nurse-Midwives: Midwives offer prenatal care and assist with uncomplicated pregnancies.

Curative Services

Various specialists: Depending on the illness or condition, patients may be treated by specialists such as cardiologists, oncologists, orthopedic surgeons, etc. General Practitioners/Family Physicians: They often serve as the first point of contact for patients seeking curative services.

Chronic Disease Services

Specialists: Patients with chronic diseases may see specialists like endocrinologists (for diabetes), cardiologists (for heart disease), or nephrologists (for kidney disease). Primary Care Physicians: Family doctors or general practitioners play a role in managing chronic diseases through ongoing care, medication management, and referrals.

Safe and Effective Medicines:

Pharmacists: Pharmacists are responsible for dispensing safe and effective medications, providing medication counseling, and ensuring drug interactions are minimal. Pharmaceutical Regulators: Government agencies and organizations regulate the pharmaceutical industry to ensure the safety and efficacy of medicines.
